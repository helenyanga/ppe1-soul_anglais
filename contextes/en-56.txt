   Your piece asks, “What’s your soul here to do?” In 1992, 34 years ago,
   the Indigo Girls 'Galileo' song asked, “How long until my soul gets it
   right?” Both questions carry the same ache. They assume the soul is
   somewhere in process — searching, learning, missing, correcting, trying
   to arrive. And emotionally, that feels true. Life often feels like
   repetition. We circle the same patterns, the same wounds, the same
   longing, the same unfinished lessons, and it can feel as though the
   soul is taking a very long time to become what it came here to be.

   But I would answer it differently. I am not sure the soul is confused.
   I am not sure the soul is late. I am not sure the soul is trying to
   “get it right” or on time.

   My experience when spinal cords are severed, legs are amputated, and
   vision robbed by sexual assault....is that the soul is consciousness:
   the observing field within us. It witnesses the life we are living, the
   ego we are believing, the patterns we are repeating, and the center we
--
   organize differently and lawfully.

   So maybe the soul’s work is not to find the perfect purpose. Maybe the
   soul is here to guide the human being toward becoming humane. Not as a
   destination. As a potential. And once that potential is realized, it
   expresses itself naturally. It may express as teaching, art, parenting,
   healing, building, friendship, leadership, service, or quiet presence.
   But those are expressions. They are not the soul itself.

   This is where the question shifts for me. Not: What is my soul here to
   do? And not even: How long until my soul gets it right? But: How long
   until I stop asking ego to lead what only the heart can guide and
   consciousness can observe?

   heart is reinstated as our compass, purpose becomes expression.

   So the soul is not here to find itself. It is here to witness and guide
   the human being back into right or lawful orientation, so what is most
   humane in each of us can finally be lived and expressed. A tree, trees.
