Profound Sayings About The Soul From Great Minds

   The concept of the soul has ancient roots tracing back to various
   perspectives held by philosophers, theologians, and
   scientists. Philosophers may view it as a manifestation of
   consciousness, while theologians see it as an eternal spiritual
   entity.

   Scientists, particularly Dr. Duncan Macdougall, attempted a more
   tangible method. His 1907 experiment involved weighing dying patients
   to ascertain the soul's weight. Remarkably, he documented a sudden
   weight loss of roughly 21 grams at death. However, his method has
   garnered skepticism.

   These views show how our exploration of the meaning of the soul will
   continue eternally. Next up we feature some more philosophical quotes
   about the soul from renowned thinkers.

     "Astronomy compels the soul to look upwards and leads us from this
     world to another."
     - Plato

     "The soul becomes dyed with the color of its thoughts."
     - Marcus Aurelius

     "The soul of man is immortal and imperishable."
     - Plato

     "I think death is nothing more than the separation of two things
     from one another: soul and body."
     - Plato

     "Happiness resides not in possessions, and not in gold, happiness
     dwells in the soul."
     - Democritus

     "Egoism is the very essence of a noble soul."
     - Friedrich Nietzsche

     "What the soul sees and has experienced, that it knows; the rest is
     appearance, prejudice and opinion."
     - Sri Aurobindo

     Never be in a hurry; do everything quietly and in a calm spirit. Do
     not lose your inner peace for anything whatsoever, even if your
     whole world seems upset.

     ~ Saint Francis de Sales

Noteworthy Soul Excerpts In Literature

   Literature has long been an avenue for exploring the intricacies of the
   soul. Poe's melancholy ode to a lost love in "The Raven '' speaks of a
   despairing soul. Meanwhile, Dostoevsky in "The Brothers Karamazov ''
   defends the idea of the enduring human spirit.

   In this section on the arts and literature, we feature soul quotes from
   various authors from their works, both historical and contemporary.

     Shall be lifted Nevermore!"

     the immortality of the human soul."

     "Hope is the thing with feathers that perches in the soul - and

     - Emily Dickinson

     "A beautiful woman delights the eye; a wise woman, the
     understanding; a pure one, the soul."
     - Minna Antrim

     "The body is subject to the force of gravity. But the soul is ruled

     - Saul Bellow

     "The human being is a most curious creature. He thinks he has got
     one soul, and he has got dozens."
     - D. H. Lawrence

     "You and I are essentially infinite choice-makers. In every moment
     of our existence, we are in that field of all possibilities where we
     have access to an infinity of choices."
     - Deepak Chopra

Insights On The Importance Of Soul In Art

   Art, in its most sincere form, expresses the artist's soul. Michael
   Jackson believed in the healing power of music that genuinely reaches
   into the soul. At the same time, Van Gogh dedicated his entire being,
   even his sanity, to his art. What is your favorite music or painting?
   Did it evoke something within your soul? This section of quotes is all
   about the soul in art to inspire the joys of life and compassion for
   other souls.

     "Beautiful music is the art of the prophets that can calm the
     agitations of the soul; it is one of the most magnificent and
     delightful presents God has given us."
     - Martin Luther

     "You use a glass mirror to see your face; you use works of art to
     see your soul."
     - George Bernard Shaw

     "Music is a mantra that soothes the soul. It's therapeutic. It's
     something our body has to have. It's very important to understand
     the power of music."
     - Michael Jackson

     "I put my heart and my soul into my work, and have lost my mind in
     the process."
     - Vincent Van Gogh

     I was always an unusual girl. My mother told me I had a chameleon
     soul, no moral compass pointing due north, no fixed personality;
     just an inner indecisiveness that was as wide and as wavering as the
     ocean.

     ~ Lana Del Rey

     Spirituality is seeded, germinates, sprouts and blossoms in the
     mundane. It is to be found and nurtured in the smallest of daily
     activities.

     ~ Thomas Moore (Care For the Soul)
