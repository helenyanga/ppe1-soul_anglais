Regional soul food

   [edit]
--
   Gullah people in the lowcountry.^[75]

   These are more specific regional soul food dishes. This includes dishes
   like jambalaya, gumbo, red rice and beans and other foods of the Creole
   subgroup of the Black American ethnic group. It also includes the
   dishes of the Gullah Geeche sub group of the Black American peoples.

   Chicken and waffles became a common soul food item in Black communities
   in Maryland and in Harlem, New York. During the blues and jazz era,
   musicians and singers ate at soul food restaurants located inside
   black-owned night clubs and cooks prepared chicken and waffles for
   their customers.^[116]^[117]
