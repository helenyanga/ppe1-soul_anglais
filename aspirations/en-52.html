Significance of Soul

Glossary Concepts
Navigation: All concepts ... Starts with S ... So
The soul, a multifaceted concept, varies across traditions. Hinduism sees it as essence, distinct from qualities. Jainism views the soul (jiva) as fundamental to living beings, bound by karma, with liberation possible through detachment. Tibetan Buddhism debates its existence, some viewing it as non-existent. Vaishnavism emphasizes its desire for divine favor. Purana describes it as uniting with the supreme spirit, Shiva. Yoga considers it the pure, quality-less experiencer. Ayurveda connects soul, mind, and body. Vedanta sees it as non-different from the Lord. Dharmashastra describes it as undergoing transmigration.1

Synonyms: Spirit, Essence, Life force, Psyche, Heart, Being, Core, Anima, Inner self, Consciousness

In Italian: Anima; In Spanish: Alma; In Portugese: Alma; In Dutch: Ziel; In Finnish: Sielu; In German: Seele; In Malay: Jiwa; In Swedish: Själ; In French: Âme; In Polish: Dusza

Hindu concept of 'Soul'
